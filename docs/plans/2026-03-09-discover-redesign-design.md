# Discover Page Redesign: The Player Dashboard

## Theme & Concept

The Discover page is transforming from a traditional list of "seeds" (career paths/courses) into a highly engaging, game-like **Player Dashboard**. The aesthetic continues the "Premium Glass & Glow" design system but applies it to a personalized "character sheet" experience. This creates a sense of progression, identity, and social connection for teenage users.

## Section 1: Player Header & Identity

### 1. Avatar & Status

- **Avatar Display:** Centrally or prominently aligned at the top with a subtle glowing "halo" effect.
- **Player Title:** A dynamic title underneath representing who they currently are (e.g., "Level 3 Explorer" or "Aspiring UX Designer").

### 2. The Ikigai Compass

- **Visual:** A sleek 2x2 grid (or quadrant) representing the 4 pillars of Ikigai (Passion, Mission, Vocation, Profession). It replaces the complex 4-circle Venn diagram with an easy-to-read, premium card design.
- **Insights:** A small section beneath the grid featuring AI-generated, game-like insights (e.g., "Your strength lies in Passion and Mission!").

## Section 2: Skills Inventory (The Tech Tree)

- **Concept:** Moving away from standard lists, skills are displayed like an RPG inventory.
- **Visual Grouping:** Grouped by category (e.g., Design, Code, Soft Skills).
- **Presentation:** Rendered as visually distinct badges, chips, or nodes.
- **Progression:** Integration of visual progress rings or proficiency tags (e.g., "Beginner", "Intermediate", "Master") so the user feels they are actively leveling up their real-world capabilities.

## Section 3: Activity & Achievements Feed

- **Concept:** A scrolling, multiplayer-style activity feed that grounds the user in a learning community.
- **Personal Unlocks:** Shows milestones the user has reached ("Unlocked the Advanced Prototyping skill").
- **Social Activity:** Shows achievements and progress updates from friends ("Alex just completed their first project").
- **Layout:** Positioned at the bottom of the dashboard, giving a continuous sense of life and movement on the app.

## Data Model (Mock vs. Real)

- Given that the backend models for "friends" and a full "skills inventory" may not be fully mature, the initial implementation will rely on structured mock data to establish the design and user flow before hooking into real endpoints later.
